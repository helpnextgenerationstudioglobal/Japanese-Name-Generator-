The Deep History of Japanese Surnames and Full Names

In Japanese culture, surnames are usually written before the given name. This tool follows that structure and generates Japanese last names combined with first names. Historically, Japanese surnames were a privilege reserved for the nobility and samurai classes. It was only during the Meiji Era that the general population began adopting surnames, often choosing names based on their local geography.

Because of this geographical origin, you will see many names in our Japanese Full Name Generator that refer to rice fields (Ta), mountains (Yama), and bridges (Hashi).
